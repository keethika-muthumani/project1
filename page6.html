Diet plan:
  1. Soft Diet Options: During severe flare-ups or when joint pain is intense, focus on softer foods that are easier to chew and swallow:
    • Soups
    • Smoothies
    • Mashed vegetables or fruits
    • Cooked grains and cereals
    • Yogurt or soft cheeses
  2. Avoid Trigger Foods: Some foods may exacerbate inflammation and worsen symptoms. Common trigger foods include:
    • Processed foods high in sugar and trans fats
    • Foods high in saturated fats (red meat, full-fat dairy)
    • Refined carbohydrates (white bread, pastries, sugary cereals)

    • Dairy
    • Shellfish
    • Nuts
  4. Stay Hydrated: Ensure adequate hydration by drinking plenty of water, herbal teas, and clear soups.
  5. Small, Frequent Meals: Eating smaller, more frequent meals can help manage energy levels and prevent fatigue.

    specializing in autoimmune diseases.

Physical actvities
  1. Gentle Range of Motion Exercises: Focus on gentle movements to maintain joint flexibility without exacerbating pain or inflammation
    • Slow, controlled stretches
    • Passive range of motion exercises (assisted by a physical therapist if needed)
    • Mindful breathing exercises to promote relaxation and reduce tension
  2. Water-Based Actvities: Take advantage of the buoyancy and support provided by water to engage in gentle exercise with minimal impact on the joints:
    • Aquatic therapy
    • Water walking or gentle swimming
    • Water aerobics classes tailored for individuals with arthritis
  3. Adapted Yoga or Tai Chi: Participate in modified yoga or tai chi classes that emphasize gentle movements, mindfulness, and breath awareness:
    • Chair yoga or seated tai
    • Gentle stretching and breathing exercises performed at a slow pace
    • Emphasis on relaxation and stress reduction
  4. Physical Therapy: Work with a physical therapist to develop an individualized exercise program that addresses specific limitations, goals, and concerns related to RA.
  5. Pacing and Rest: Listen to your body and pace yourself during physical actvity. Allow for adequate rest periods and modify actvities as needed to avoid overexertion.
  6. Consult Healthcare Professionals: Always consult with your rheumatologist or primary care physician before starting any new exercise program, especially during flare-ups or periods of increased disease actvity.
